Know the Signs

Warning Signs of Suicide Risk

Recognising these signs in yourself or someone you love is the first step toward getting help. If you notice these, reach out immediately.

Talking about wanting to die or to kill oneself

Looking for ways to kill oneself — searching online or acquiring means

Talking about feeling hopeless or having no reason to live

Talking about feeling trapped or being in unbearable pain

Increasing use of alcohol or drugs

Withdrawing from friends, family, and community

Extreme mood swings or sudden calmness after deep distress

Giving away prized possessions

Sleeping too little or too much

Displaying extreme agitation or reckless behaviour

Saying goodbyes as if they will not be seen again

Dramatic changes in behaviour, appearance, or appetite

Self-Help Tools

Evidence-Based Coping Techniques

These strategies are based on clinical best practices. Use them to manage stress, anxiety, and overwhelming emotions.

Box Breathing (4-4-4-4)
  1. Inhale slowly through your nose for 4 seconds
  2. Hold your breath for 4 seconds
  3. Exhale slowly through your mouth for 4 seconds
  4. Hold for 4 seconds
  5. Repeat 4–6 times until calm returns
5-4-3-2-1 Grounding
  • Name 5 things you can see
  • Name 4 things you can physically feel
  • Name 3 things you can hear
  • Name 2 things you can smell
  • Name 1 thing you can taste
Thought Reframing (CBT)
  1. Write down the negative thought
  2. Identify the emotion it triggers
  3. Find evidence for and against it
  4. Write a more balanced, realistic thought
  5. Notice how your mood shifts
Progressive Muscle Relaxation
  1. Sit or lie in a comfortable position
  2. Tense feet and calves for 5 seconds, then release
  3. Work up through thighs, abdomen, hands, arms, shoulders
  4. Scrunch your face muscles for 5 seconds, release
  5. Notice the deep relaxation throughout your body
